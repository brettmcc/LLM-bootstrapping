## 1/13/2025 meeting notes

Core question: how to quantify the uncertainty of a parameter's estimate induced by various potential (reasonable) researcher choices in filtering data, constructing variables, and choosing the specification?

- From the perspective of statistical decision theory, planner wants to see all possible REASONABLE robustness checks (in terms of sample, variables, specification) and see how the parameter varies to quantify the uncertainty before making a decision. 

But assessing the reasonableness of a vector of (sample, variables, spec) choices is nontrivial. Could ask an LLM to do so, but would then want to compare that assessment(s) to a set of human assessments. 

Idea of replicating Nick Huntington-Klein's ~150 attempts to answer a single research questions is to (i) assume the human economists in his study were acting reasonably, then (ii) see whether LLMs can match it. If so, then we could take LLM to other contexts and use it as a pseudo-economist replicator $N$ times to see how a 'reasonable' economist would replicate one's own research question.
